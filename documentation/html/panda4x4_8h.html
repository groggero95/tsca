◆ ATTACK_MUL

#define ATTACK_MUL   1

Activate the attack on the multiply step.

The attack is only possible on this step thus we need to leave it to 1.
This option has been implemented only for demonstration porposes.

◆ ATTACK_SQUARE

#define ATTACK_SQUARE   1

Activate the attack on the squaring step.

Define if during the attack we want to estimate also the time taken by the sqyuaring step.
In practice if activated it gives a non zero value to the pcc on a zero bit guess on the exponent.
Without its contribution the attack may require from 3 to 5 times the amount of messages.

◆ B_CONSIDERED

#define B_CONSIDERED

Number of bit of the exponent considered at each iteration of the attack.

This constant determine the amount of guess that will be made for the next B_CONSIDERED bits.
All these guesses will be tested using the pcc and only B_GUESSED will be consolidated.
The bigger this number the slower the attack will execute, since the amount of guess depends exponentially on it.

◆ B_GUESSED

#define B_GUESSED

Number of bit of the exponent guessed at each iteration of the attack.

This constant determine the amount of bit that I will consolidate as known at each iteration of the attack.
In order for the attack to work we need: 

B_CONSIDERED >= B_GUESSED

Moreover as the diference increase between these two constant the more confidence we have on the bit thta we are guessing.

◆ COEFF

#define COEFF   3

Coefficent used in filtering.

Define how many times, with respect to the standard deviation, a sample must be displaced from the mean value of the distribution in order to be discarded by the fitering function.

Remarks
Filtering is only relevant on the case of noisy measure derived from time acquisitions made on an operating system

◆ msg_t

typedef struct msg_t msg_t

Structure to hold information needed during the attack.

This structure is used to hold the intermediate values c and s inside of a modular exponentiation.
It also has a member to store the time estimate of the current and past iteration.
